The Brief
///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

Eels are the long-running indie rock project of songwriter Mark Oliver Everett, known as E, with a catalogue stretching back to 1996. Earth to Dora is their thirteenth studio album, released in 2020.

I created three lyric videos for the album, each built around a fully rigged and animated version of one of the characters from the album's illustration world, with a style drawing from classic mid-century cartoons in the tradition of Hanna-Barbera. In each case I took the flat artwork and rebuilt the character as a fully animatable rig.

Baby Let's Make It Real centres on a cigar-smoking baby in a high chair, with lyrics materialising out of puffs of smoke that build from a wisp to full lettering. The world around the baby grows increasingly chaotic as the song builds: floating musical notes, a marching band, halos, hearts and confetti, all crashing together in a big finale.

Anything for Boo follows a cheeky ghost on a search for love: floating around, playing with heart balloons, strolling across the Abbey Road crossing, contemplating with a tiny angel and devil on his shoulders, and finally chasing a glowing heart through a night-time forest.

Who You Say You Are takes a film noir approach, placing a world-weary detective under a flickering streetlight as lyrics are revealed by a roving spotlight. Footprints appear across the scene, rain falls, and visual elements tied directly to the lyrics come and go: a spinning globe, a set of scales, mathematical equations, a broken neon heart. The video ends on a quiet starry sky, fading to black.
