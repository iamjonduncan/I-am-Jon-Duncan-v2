The Brief
///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

Dance Fever is Florence and the Machine's fifth studio album, rooted in Welch's fascination with choreomania, a social phenomenon in early modern Europe where groups of people would dance erratically and uncontrollably. It debuted at number one in the UK.

I created three lyric videos for the album: Search and Destroy, Dream Girl Evil, and Mermaids. Working from the existing artwork as a starting point, each video builds out into a fully realised world in the same visual language: embroidered tapestry figures, lace borders, and illuminated manuscript imagery brought to life through animation. A custom font built from an ancient tapestry carries the lyrics, set inside scrolling ribbon banners that feel pulled directly from the fabric of the artwork itself. Each track gets its own distinct scene: storm-lit lightning bolts for Search and Destroy, serpents and winged figures for Dream Girl Evil, and a mythological mermaid rising from a misty seascape for Mermaids.
